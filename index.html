Imitations of Birds and Other Things

"All Rivers Are Alluvial" (6/3/2025)

Oxbows halve flows that still live on their own.
Drips and drops a young stream a brook a creek
ticks and tocks across a loamy flick of sod of God
hark sizzles of foam left leftward or wayward
sharp trickle toward toward towards

Drizzle irrigates itself clay motes and seed pods
conjoin and drop off wide turns and new plots
into new pots and pans and plods and lops another gold
oxbow’s flickering tracks alongside the river which
glints in its own way onward onward onwards

On and on each flood whittles more earth,
Each drought bakes it. The creek’s a canyon.
Ceaseless flow if it knows what it knows.
Forks meander; rivers rush and forget their flakes.
The river carves and slakes its own settling path.
Time ticks and locks.

All running rivers run to the sea,
but what did the gold panner see in me?

"Birdwatching With My Daughter"

Entangled turnstiles of boughs
through which the wood thrush,
climbing the length of the dawn,
fast as quanta - light as an instant -
rushes seen and unseen by me and Sylvia.

Inside, I hold my coffee and my baby,
yawning through dawn, catching
birds to learn together.

Look,
see the bird?

And the thrush - and the thrush - are gone.
Sylvia looks at me, anyway.

"A Shade of Light" (2/7/2023 ~10:40pm)

There is a shade of light
From perimeters
That shivers through a downy cloud,
Unbuttoning the dusky curtain,
Over woods and fenced-in field.

To see it waft from darkled height,
And lap the shaded field,
Forms patterns in the mind at night-
Paintings of the yield.

There is a shade of light
From the wick that circumscribes.

"To Be a Father" (3:47 New Year’s Day)

It is raining outside,
A multiplicity of drips forken more drops,
Pitter patter little hooves on pane for me,
Inside, sweating a pool of ticks and tocks,
and waiting:
For if it shows I cannot go,
To which I sow if four can know.

He, oaken elm full fat bodied,
Chalk withering lick theft, I,
Sheltered from the storm,
In the helm hollow hutch and whisper
Please please please
A dew a dew
I say
To you
